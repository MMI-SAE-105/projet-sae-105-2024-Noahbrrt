FIFA 1904-2004: A Century of Football

A Dive into FIFA's History

The book "FIFA 1904-2004: A Century of Football" chronicles a century of football history through the birth and evolution of FIFA (Fédération Internationale de Football Association). Published on FIFA's centenary, this book provides a detailed retrospective on how the organization shaped modern football and helped make it the most popular sport in the world. It traces FIFA's modest origins in 1904, founded in Paris by a few European federations, to its journey in transforming football into a global phenomenon.

Throughout its pages, the book highlights key moments in football history, from the first major competitions such as the World Cup and the Olympic Games, to the iconic figures who marked the sport's evolution. From legendary players to influential coaches, "FIFA 1904-2004" is a true ode to the legacy of football and the social and cultural impact the sport has had worldwide. This book is enriched with anecdotes, statistics, and archive photographs that make it a captivating read for football enthusiasts.

Football as a Global Phenomenon

One of the book's main themes is how FIFA contributed to making football a global phenomenon. By promoting the sport beyond European borders, FIFA managed to make football accessible to millions of people, encouraging the creation of federations in emerging countries and launching international competitions that brought nations together around the sport.

The book also highlights how football has become a universal language, allowing people to connect, share common values, and build a collective identity around this sport. "FIFA 1904-2004" shows that beyond mere entertainment, football has become a vector for social change and a means of expression for millions of fans worldwide. Through the World Cup, FIFA has created events that transcend sports to become genuine cultural and national gatherings.

From a Simple Object to a Global Sport

It traces the evolution of football, starting from a simple object, the ball, to becoming a global sport. This book shows how FIFA structured and popularized football on an international scale, influencing entire societies. Moreover, FIFA's establishment of the rules of the game echoes the creation of new rules and traditions like the Bochiball tribe. The book also highlights football's cultural and social impact, illustrating how this sport unites people—an interesting parallel to the tribe's transformation.
